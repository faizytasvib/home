Rome was founded around 625 BC in the areas of ancient Italy known as Etruria and Latium. The most famous legend regarding its founding is of two brothers, and demigods, Romulus and Remus. Apparently, in an argument over who would rule the city, Romulus killed Remus and named the city after himself.

The first period in Roman history is known as the Period of Kings. These kings built a very successful military and economic base, which helped the Roman Empire prosper. When Rome entered its Republican Period, it was no longer ruled by kings. Instead it had established a new form of government, in which the higher class ruled (senators, equestrians, knights).

Rome continued to expand through the Republican Period. Soon, Rome fell into war but was shifted to Imperialism began when Julius Caesar rose to power.
